Carl Sagan Tribute Page

In this pale blue dot

1934 - 1996

Astronomer · Cosmologist · Astrophysicist · Author,

Son of Samuel Sagan, an emigrant worker from the present Ukraine who during the Depression worked as a theater usher, and Rachel Molly Gruber, a housewife in New York. He had a sister, Carol, and the family lived in a modest apartment near the Atlantic Ocean, in Bensonhurst, a neighborhood of Brooklyn.

In addition to scientific publications, he wrote several popular books, the most famous are:

The Dragons of Eden (1977) Pulitzer Prize
Cosmos (1980)
Contact (1985)

"Science is much more than a body of knowledge. It is a way of thinking. This is central to its success. Science invites us to let the facts in, even when they don’t conform to our preconceptions.."

"Why We Need To Understand Science" in The Skeptical Inquirer Vol. 14, Issue 3 (Spring 1990)
